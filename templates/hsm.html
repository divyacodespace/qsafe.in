Why an HSM matters

  • Private keys never leave the tamper-evident boundary; signing happens inside the module.
  • FIPS 140-3 / Common Criteria validation supports PCI-DSS 4.0 and ISO 27001 control objectives.
  • Hybrid classical + ML-KEM/ML-DSA pilots are issued under the same HSM-protected root.
  • Audit-grade key-use logs feed straight into the agent's audit trail.

Recommended operational flow

  1. Generate root CA inside the HSM at 4096-bit RSA or ML-DSA-87, never exportable.
  2. Issue intermediate CAs under the HSM-protected root; pin them in the agent.
  3. Sign all leaf CSRs through the HSM; record the operation hash to the audit log.
  4. Rotate keys per PCI-DSS 4.0 §3.7 — auto-trigger via the agent ≤ 45 days before expiry.
  5. Maintain a quantum-safe parallel hierarchy and migrate by service tier.
